planes

An airplane is a powered, fixed-wing aircraft that glides through the air using thrust. It relies on four fundamental aerodynamic forces: lift, weight, thrust, and drag. Since the Wright brothers' first flight in 1903, planes have revolutionized global travel, providing the fastest and safest mode of long-distance transportation for billions of passengers.

animals

Animals are an incredibly diverse group of multicellular, eukaryotic organisms that inhabit nearly every corner of the planet. They play crucial roles in maintaining ecological balance, supporting human livelihoods, and providing companionship. Because they are heterotrophic, they rely on consuming other organic matter for energy and possess the remarkable ability to move independently.

space

Space begins just 62 miles (100 kilometres) above Earth's sea level at the Kármán Line. This boundary opens into an expansive vacuum filled with breathtaking celestial bodies, including stars, planets, and galaxies. Although it appears empty, this boundless frontier is constantly shaped by gravity, radiation, and the mysteries of dark matter.
